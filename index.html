About me

I'm pursuing a Master in City Planning at MIT, with a concentration in Environmental Planning and Policy. Prior to that, I taught and supported research using Geographic Information Systems (GIS) at the Smith College Spatial Analysis Lab. I graduated from Middlebury College in 2015, where I jointly majored in Geography and Environmental Studies and minored in African Studies.

Having lived in New England for the past six years, I’ve seen firsthand the burgeoning effects of climate change, from shorter ski seasons to Hurricane Irene and most recently the bomb cyclone. These experiences have fostered my passion for climate change resilience, and how the process of preparing for the inevitable effects of climate change such as sea level rise, extreme weather, new diseases, and new ecological patterns offers an opportunity to build sustainability into our policies and practices. Specifically, I’m interested in how data science, visualization, and public engagement can make this process faster, more efficient, and more equitable.

Recent Projects

Building Back Somerville's Urban Forest

A participatory data sculpture to raise awareness about the lack of trees and their many benefits in Union Square, Somerville, MA. Final project for Data Storytelling Studio at the MIT Media Lab, Spring 2018.

Between the Lines

Examines the organizational and political context of three different flood models used for climate resilience planning in East Boston. Submitted to the 2017-18 Climate Changed competition and selected for exhibition in the School of Architecture and Planning's Keller Gallery.

What is a Smart City?

My attempt to defeine this trendy but nebulous term.

The Other B-word

Investigates the gendered use of the word "bossy" in Guardian articles. Midterm project for MAS.S70: Applied Data Visualization.

When In Rome

An interactive complement to the Smith College Museum of Art's 2016 exhibition When In Rome: 1550-1900.

Carillon Map

Map of carillons (bell towers) in North America. Part of the Smith College Spatial Analysis Lab's Mystery Maps series.
