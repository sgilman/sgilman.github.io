About me

I'm am urban planner who uses data analysis, visualization, and community engagement to make our transportation systems more sustainable and more equitable. In 2019, I completed a Master in City Planning at MIT, with a concentration in Environmental Planning and Policy. Prior to that, I taught and supported research using Geographic Information Systems (GIS) at the Smith College Spatial Analysis Lab. I graduated from Middlebury College in 2015, where I jointly majored in Geography and Environmental Studies and minored in African Studies.

Portfolio

Featured Projects

@SQ9

What makes a successful public space? @SQ9 was a pop-up event that sought to measure how physical interventions can activate and improve public space. Our class transformed the plaza in front of MIT Building 9 with furniture, paint, and plants. My team trained a machine learning model to track usage of the chairs.

West Philadelphia Landscape Project

An interactive map to explore the relationship between a buried river and current social conditions in West Philadelphia. The map includes 360-degree panoramas with embedded photos and newsletters of cave-in events to contextualize the effects of the buried river in the cucrrent landscape.

Building Back Somerville's Urban Forest

A participatory data sculpture to raise awareness about the lack of trees and their many benefits in Union Square, Somerville, MA. Final project for Data Storytelling Studio at the MIT Media Lab, Spring 2018.

Polar Jenga

A reimagined Jenga that makes it fun to learn about and take action on climate change.

Between the Lines

Examines the organizational and political context of three different flood models used for climate resilience planning in East Boston. Submitted to the 2017-18 Climate Changed competition and selected for exhibition in the School of Architecture and Planning's Keller Gallery.

What is a Smart City?

My attempt to defeine this trendy but nebulous term.

The Other B-word

Investigates the gendered use of the word "bossy" in Guardian articles. Midterm project for MAS.S70: Applied Data Visualization.

When In Rome

An interactive complement to the Smith College Museum of Art's 2016 exhibition When In Rome: 1550-1900.
